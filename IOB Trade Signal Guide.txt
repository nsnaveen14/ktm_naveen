
IOB Signal Trading Guide: From a Trader's Perspective
When an IOB Signal is DETECTED
1. Identify the IOB Type
BULLISH_IOB (Demand Zone): Price swept a low, then reversed upward with a strong BOS (Break of Structure)
BEARISH_IOB (Supply Zone): Price swept a high, then reversed downward with a strong BOS
2. Validate the Signal
Check
What to Look For
BOS Confirmation
Structure break must be clear and decisive
FVG Presence
Fair Value Gap adds confluence (higher probability)
Signal Confidence
Higher confidence = better setup
Distance to Zone
Is price far enough to give you time to prepare?
3. Plan the Trade
Entry: At zone midpoint or zone edge (conservative vs aggressive)
Stop Loss: Below zone low (bullish) or above zone high (bearish)
Targets: Use the pre-calculated T1, T2, T3 levels
Risk-Reward: Ensure R:R ≥ 1:2 minimum
4. Wait for Price to Return
Set alerts at the IOB zone
Do NOT chase price; let it come to you

When an IOB Signal is MITIGATED
1. Understand What Mitigation Means
Price has entered the IOB zone (between zoneLow and zoneHigh). This is your trade trigger.
2. Execute the Trade
BULLISH_IOB Mitigated → Look for LONG entry
BEARISH_IOB Mitigated → Look for SHORT entry
3. Confirm Entry (Optional Refinement)
Wait for a reaction candle (rejection wick, engulfing pattern)
Use lower timeframe confirmation (e.g., if IOB on 15min, confirm on 5min)
4. Manage the Position
Stage
Action
Entry
Execute at zone with proper position sizing
T1 Hit
Book partial profits (25-50%)
T2 Hit
Trail stop to entry or T1
T3 Hit
Exit remaining position
Stop Loss Hit
Accept the loss, move on
5. Mark the IOB as Used
After trade execution (win or loss), the IOB is no longer valid for re-entry.

Key Takeaways
Detection = Preparation phase (plan, size, set alerts)
Mitigation = Execution phase (trigger, enter, manage)
Never trade an IOB after it's fully mitigated without reaction
Always respect stop loss levels
